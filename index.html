Data at the bench · organism-in-environment

Multiplex bacterial interaction networks

Université Euromed de Fès — undergraduate research

A trait-resolved interaction study across 60 soil bacterial strains, generating 12 directed interaction layers from large-scale pairwise assays. A multilayer network pipeline over the resulting 3D tensor yields a 4-dimensional ecological embedding that classifies microbial strategies across antagonistic, metabolic, and morphogenetic modes.

Single-cell representation learning

Helmholtz Munich, Lücken Lab — research collaborator

An architecture combining contrastive learning and multi-head attention to learn patient-level embeddings from single-cell transcriptomics. 05

Auditing & identifiability

Causal identifiability of sparse-autoencoder features in foundation models

Sole author

A causally-grounded test of whether the interpretability “features” read out of a genomic foundation model are trustworthy, using saturation-mutagenesis MPRA for per-base-pair causal ground truth. A real aggregate causal signal survives a full control suite — yet the per-feature SAE decomposition is non-identifiable: causal features barely reproduce across retrains, a second SAE family recovers a near-disjoint causal set from identical activations (cross-family Jaccard ≈ 0.01), and a single supervised direction stably decodes the effect. The failure is the decomposition, not the model. Generalizes across two SAE families (TopK, Gated) and two architectures (HyenaDNA, Caduceus).

Sole author · preprint “A Causal Signal Without Causal Features” · target: MLCB
